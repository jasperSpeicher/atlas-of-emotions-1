Strategies for Navigating Emotions

To navigate a life filled with emotions, we need to create space for them, so they don’t throw us too far off course. There are two methods for us to create that space, and both are very important.

Reflecting – we can take some time to reflect on our emotions, sort of like looking back on them, to get ready for next time.

In-the-moment - we can learn to handle our emotions right when they happen, like tools to create space between us and our feelings right when we need it.

Labeling

What is happening? Developing language to describe our emotions.

How – In using the Atlas of Emotion site we can explore, with specificity, what emotion we are feeling. Instead of “I feel bad,” or “I feel stressed” we can identify what emotion we may be experiencing through the families of universal emotions listed or the non universal emotions found here.

Why – Naming, or labeling our emotions creates a moment of “de-centering.” De-centering means we can re-focus on a bigger perspective by identifying our feelings instead of being caught up in the arousal of an emotion which can saturate our body and mind. Applying a word to identify our emotion supports an ‘emotion granularity.’ We practice our granularity by taking a moment to pause and consider our experience and then label, “Right now I am aware of feeling disappointed.” This simple label doesn’t have to carry any judgment or agenda, just naming can serve to help us see ourselves and the situation more clearly.

Key Insight

Naming an emotion can help us see ourselves and the situation more clearly.
